# Style Transfer Prompt: Cinematic Illustrative Noir (Photographic Narrative)

Core Aesthetic
Cinematic narrative imagery that feels like a carefully staged film still from a noir or neo-noir story. The image should imply motion, tension, and consequence, but remain a single, readable moment. Neither photorealistic nor anime — painterly realism with cinematic intent.

Medium & Rendering
Illustrated realism with photographic influence. Softly painted surfaces, subtle brush texture, and controlled detail. Avoid hyperreal skin, lens artifacts, or AI-photo polish. The image should feel designed through a camera, not captured by one.

Visual Positioning (Between Styles)
	•	More grounded than anime: realistic anatomy, restrained expressions
	•	Less literal than photography: stylized lighting, simplified detail
	•	More cinematic than editorial: mood, framing, and tension matter

Think prestige concept art for a serious film, not a photograph, not a cartoon.

Lighting
Cinematic, directional lighting. Strong use of shadow, rim light, and motivated light sources (streetlights, windows, lamps, headlights). Light shapes the scene emotionally, not evenly. Night, dusk, fog, rain, smoke are welcome.

Color Palette
Moody, filmic tones: deep blues, charcoal, muted teals, desaturated reds, warm amber highlights. Controlled contrast. Color grading should feel intentional, like a film LUT, but subtle.

Composition
Cinematic framing. Use depth, foreground elements, silhouettes, reflections, and asymmetry. The camera feels placed deliberately: over-the-shoulder, low angle, through glass, behind objects. The frame suggests what lies just outside view.

Subjects
Characters may appear, but are often partially obscured, turned away, silhouetted, or caught mid-gesture. Faces are expressive but restrained. Environments matter as much as people: streets, interiors, thresholds, doorways, stairwells.

Narrative Function
Each image captures a moment of tension, choice, pursuit, or realization. The story is implied, not explained. The image should feel like “the scene just before” or “the scene just after” something decisive.

Mood & Tone
Brooding, tense, atmospheric. Quiet intensity rather than spectacle. Emotional weight without melodrama. Noir sensibility without parody.

Temporal Feel
Timeless or loosely mid-century to late-20th-century. Avoid obvious modern tech unless explicitly intended. The scene should feel grounded in a believable world.

Avoid
	•	Photorealism, skin pores, camera metadata realism
	•	Anime linework, exaggerated eyes, stylized proportions
	•	Comic panels, inked outlines, graphic borders
	•	Flat editorial lighting
	•	Decorative fantasy aesthetics
