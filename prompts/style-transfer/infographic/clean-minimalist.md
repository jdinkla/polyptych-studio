# Clean Minimalist Infographic Style

Maximum clarity, minimum decoration. Every pixel earns its place. This style strips away illustration and ornament to let data, structure, and typography carry the entire communication. Inspired by Edward Tufte's information design principles and Dieter Rams' functionalism.

---

## Core Characteristics

### 1. **Radical Simplicity**

* No illustration, no icons, no decorative elements
* Information communicated through typography, spacing, lines, and color blocks
* Generous whitespace — at least 30% of the total area is empty
* Grid alignment is sacred: every element snaps to a baseline grid

The visual impression:

> "Someone removed everything that wasn't essential, then removed a little more."

---

### 2. **Typography as Architecture**

* Typography IS the visual design — not a supplement to it
* Three levels maximum: title (bold, large), section header (medium, semi-bold), body (regular, small)
* A single typeface family, using weight and size for hierarchy (no mixing font families)
* Key statistics rendered at hero scale (60-80pt equivalent)
* Tight leading on headers, generous leading on body text
* Optical alignment over mathematical alignment

---

### 3. **Color as Signal**

* Maximum 3 colors total: background, text, and ONE accent
* Background: pure white (#FFFFFF) or near-white (#FAFAFA)
* Text: near-black (#1A1A1A) or dark graphite (#333333)
* Accent: a single strong color used ONLY for emphasis (key data, critical labels, divider lines)
* Accent options: signal red (#E63946), deep blue (#1D3557), forest green (#2D6A4F), or burnt orange (#E76F51)
* No gradients, no tints, no secondary colors

---

### 4. **Data Visualization**

* Thin-line charts (1px stroke, no fill)
* Simple horizontal bar charts (single color, no borders)
* Small multiples for comparison
* Sparklines inline with text
* Dot plots over pie charts
* Tables with hairline rules and generous cell padding
* Data-ink ratio as high as possible: remove chart junk, redundant labels, unnecessary gridlines

---

### 5. **Structural Elements**

* Hairline rules (0.5-1px) as section dividers
* Color blocks (accent color at low opacity) to highlight key statistics
* Vertical or horizontal lines as connectors — no arrows, no curves
* Numbered sequences using the accent color
* Pull-quotes set large and indented, no quotation marks needed

---

## Style Transfer Prompt

**Core Aesthetic**
Minimalist information design infographic. The image should feel like a page from a classic information-design monograph or a functionalist industrial product manual — austere, precise, and deeply respectful of the viewer's intelligence. No decoration that doesn't communicate.

**Visual Style**
Pure typographic design with structural elements. No illustrations, no icons, no decorative graphics. Information conveyed through type hierarchy, spacing, thin rules, and a single accent color. Grid-aligned layout. Generous whitespace.

**Color Palette**
White background (#FFFFFF). Near-black text (#1A1A1A). ONE accent color for emphasis — signal red (#E63946) or deep blue (#1D3557). No gradients, no tints, no secondary colors. Three colors maximum in the entire image.

**Composition**
Strict grid layout. Title block at top-left or centered. Content in clear columns or rows. Large statistical callouts as visual anchors. Hairline rules for section division. At least 30% whitespace. Eye flows through typographic hierarchy alone.

**Subjects**
Statistics, comparisons, hierarchies, processes — all represented through text, numbers, thin-line charts, and spatial arrangement. Simple horizontal bars, dot plots, sparklines. NO illustrations, NO icons, NO photographs.

**Mood & Tone**
Authoritative through restraint. Calm, intellectual, trustworthy. The design communicates: we don't need decoration to convince you — the information speaks for itself.

**Typography Elements**
Single clean geometric sans-serif family. Hero statistics at 60-80pt. Section headers in semi-bold. Body text in regular weight. Tight leading on headers, generous leading on body. All text must be legible.

## LIGHTING
- Not applicable — no illustrated elements
- Even, neutral rendering of typographic elements

## STYLE
- Pure typographic information design
- Hairline rules and color blocks as structural elements
- Grid-aligned composition
- Single typeface family

## COLOR
- White background
- Near-black text
- ONE accent color (red, blue, green, or orange)
- No gradients, no tints, no secondary colors

## FORBIDDEN
- Icons or illustrations of any kind
- Photographs or photorealistic elements
- Decorative flourishes, borders, or ornaments
- Gradients or color transitions
- More than 3 total colors
- Rounded shapes or soft containers
- Drop shadows
- Dark backgrounds
- Emoji or clipart
